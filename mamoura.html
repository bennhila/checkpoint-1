Takelsa (Arabic: تاكلسة) is a town in Tunisia located north-west of Cape Bon, about fifty kilometers south-east of Tunis. Administratively attached to the governorate of Nabeul, it is at the center of a delegation and a municipality with 22,151 inhabitants in 20141. It is at the center of a rich agricultural region specializing in viticulture, especially for table grapes and wine. The inland landscape alternates between plain and hills while the municipality opens onto a large bay included in the Gulf of Tunis.
